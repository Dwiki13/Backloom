# KII Business Analysis & Recommendations

Forward-looking business analysis for all three businesses. Not just recording — interpreting data and recommending actions.

## Analysis Framework

When analyzing ANY business, always cover:

### 1. Financial Health Check
- Revenue trend (up/down/stable)
- Expense trend (fixed vs variable costs)
- Profit margin (current vs target)
- Cash runway (months until burnout)
- Payroll ratio (payroll/revenue)

### 2. Operational Efficiency
- Utilization rate (Rental PS: occupancy, DevLokal: project pipeline)
- Cost per output (DevLokal: cost per project, Rental PS: cost per rental hour)

### 3. Growth Signals
- Revenue growth MoM
- New client acquisition rate
- Market indicators (Google Ads performance, competitor pricing)
- Capacity constraints

### 4. Risk Assessment
- Single-client dependency
- Cash flow gaps
- Market risks (new competitors, price wars)
- Operational risks (employee turnover, equipment failure)

## Multi-Business Portfolio Analysis

### Diversification Health
- No single business should be > 50% of total income
- If one business dies, can others cover fixed costs?

### Cash Allocation Strategy
- Which business has best ROI for additional investment?
- Where to cut vs where to invest more?
- Shared resources across businesses

### Growth Priority Matrix

| | High Revenue | Low Revenue |
|---|---|---|
| **High Margin** | INVEST MORE | MAINTAIN |
| **Low Margin** | OPTIMIZE | CUT/RESTRUCTURE |

## Analysis Workflow

When running an analysis:

1. **Load devlokal-accounting skill** — ensure data is current
2. **Read all data files** from all three businesses
3. **Calculate key metrics** per business + combined
4. **Compare against benchmarks** above
5. **Identify top 3 opportunities + top 3 risks**
6. **Generate recommendations** with estimated financial impact
7. **Save analysis** to `/root/projects/analysis-YYYY-MM.md`
8. **Send summary** to Topic 3 (Bisnis)

### Style Rules

- **Keep it concise** — KII prefers "segitu dulu aja" style. Don't over-explain.
- Give the analysis, key numbers, and top recommendations. Skip fluff.
- Use bullet lists, not verbose paragraphs.
- Language: ID/EN mix, casual KII style
- When presenting analysis, lead with the numbers and recommendations, not the methodology
