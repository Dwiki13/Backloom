## Session: June 21, 2026 — Prototype-First Approach

### Context
KII and OWL redesigned the WA outreach approach:
- **Old:** Long intro template → FU1 send portfolio → FU2
- **New:** Send prototype link directly + 1 sentence personal → FU1 → FU2

### Why Prototype-First Works Better
1. People don't read long WA messages — they scan 2-3 lines
2. A prototype link is worth 100 words of description
3. Faster: 1-2 min per contact vs 3-5 min
4. Better filter: only people who click and respond are qualified
5. Response rate expected: 10% → 25-35%

### Real-World Example: LUXE LAUNDRY (June 21, 2026)

**Situation:** Lead said "hmm..ok sy pikirkan dulu yaa. Tks" — never received portfolio or pricing before.

**Template used (prototype-first):**
```
Halo, ini contoh website laundry yang pernah saya buat:
https://laundry-website-desi-pguf.bolt.host/

Kalau cocok, bisa saya buatkan yang serupa untuk [nama laundry] 😊
```

**Key insight:** When lead has NEVER received portfolio + pricing, send prototype directly in the first follow-up. Don't ask "mau lihat portfolio?" — just send it.

### Real-World Example: King Laundry (June 21, 2026)

**Situation:** Lead asked "Brp website gtu ka?", received detailed quote + SEO pitch including specific Google Maps observation, no response.

**Template used:**
```
Halo Pak/Bu [nama laundry], maaf ganggu lagi 🙏

Saya Dwiki dari DevLokal. Sebelumnya sudah saya kirim info soal website + SEO untuk laundry di Mustika Raya Bekasi.

Mau saya kirim contoh website laundry yang sudah saya buat? Biar bisa dilihat langsung tampilannya seperti apa.

Kalau berminat, bisa langsung saya bantu buatkan. Prosesnya cepat, 1-2 hari jadi 👍
```

**Key insight:** When lead already received pricing but went silent, follow-up should offer VISUAL proof (portfolio/screenshot), not more text. They already have the info — they need to SEE it.

### Key Insights
- **"Pikirkan dulu" ≠ lost lead** — it means "not ready yet, but interested enough to not say no"
- **Portfolio/screenshots > text descriptions** — for leads who asked price, send visual proof in follow-up
- **Reference their specific business** — "laundry di Mustika Raya Bekasi" shows you remember them
- **Don't offer discount in first follow-up** — signals desperation, reduces perceived value
- **Give an easy out** — "kabari kalau sudah siap" reduces pressure and keeps the door open

### Follow-Up Sequence Timing
- Intro: Day 0 (send prototype link directly)
- Follow-up #1: Day 3 (gentle reminder)
- Follow-up #2: Day 7 (final attempt, permission to say no)
- Max 2 follow-ups after intro

## General Rules for Indonesian Service Businesses

1. **WA is the primary sales channel** — not email, not forms
2. **Response time < 5 minutes** during business hours
3. **Auto-reply** for after-hours messages
4. **Max 2 follow-ups** after intro — after that, mark as lost
5. **Track leads** in spreadsheet or WA Business labels (New → Contacted → Follow-up → Closed/Lost)
6. **Prototype-first** for cold outreach — send link directly, skip long intro
